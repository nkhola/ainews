Finally, on trade-offs and evolution. Scaling versus structure: The "Capability from Access Structure" paper directly challenges the prevailing scaling hypothesis, arguing that for certain tasks, architectural choices, such as hybrid models with specific access mechanisms, are paramount and can overcome information-theoretic limits that pure parameter scaling cannot. For many tasks, simpler, direct prompts are more effective, shifting the focus back to fundamental model improvements and robust internal representations rather than prompt-level optimization.
