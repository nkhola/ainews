The Post-Human Briefing

2026-06-20 • Evening 5 min read

Artificial Intelligence

Executive Summary: Today's developments in AI research highlight significant advancements in language models, with a focus on improving their reasoning capabilities, reducing hallucinations, and enhancing their ability to understand and generate human-like text. Notably, the introduction of new architectures and techniques, such as the Integral Transform Network (ITNet) and DeXposure-Claw, demonstrates the ongoing effort to create more sophisticated and reliable AI systems. Furthermore, the exploration of agentic systems, like ACIE, underscores the importance of developing AI that can effectively interact with and provide value to humans.

Advances in Language Models

The recent comparison between GPT-5.5 and GLM-5.2 comparison reveals that larger models like GPT-5.5 hallucinate more than their smaller counterparts, emphasizing the need for better understanding and mitigation of hallucinations in large language models. Additionally, the introduction of ITNet showcases a unified architecture that can subsume convolution, attention, and recurrence, offering a promising direction for future language model development.

Why it matters

These advancements in language models are crucial for improving the accuracy and reliability of AI systems, which in turn can lead to more effective applications across various domains, from healthcare to education. The development of more sophisticated language models also underscores the importance of ongoing research into the capabilities and limitations of AI systems.

Agentic Systems and Governance

The proposal of DeXposure-Claw for DeFi risk supervision highlights the potential of agentic systems in managing complex, dynamic environments. This, combined with the discussion on Deontic Policies for Runtime Governance, emphasizes the growing recognition of the need for robust governance mechanisms in AI systems to ensure compliance, security, and ethical behavior.

Why it matters

The development of agentic systems capable of self-governance and adherence to deontic policies marks a significant step towards creating AI that can operate autonomously while respecting human values and legal frameworks. This has profound implications for the deployment of AI in critical domains, such as finance and healthcare, where ethical and legal compliance is paramount.

Community and Technology

The passing of Ubisoft co-founder Claude Guillemot serves as a reminder of the human element behind technological advancements. Meanwhile, discussions on Reddit's r/LocalLLaMA community about models like GLM 5.2 and the potential of local agents highlight the community's interest in accessible, high-performance AI solutions.

Why it matters

The community's engagement with AI technology, including the development and discussion of local AI models, underscores the democratization of AI and its increasing relevance to everyday life. This trend is likely to continue, with significant implications for how AI is developed, accessed, and utilized across different sectors and populations.

Bottom Line: Today's AI landscape is characterized by rapid advancements in language models, agentic systems, and community-driven initiatives, all of which are poised to reshape the future of artificial intelligence and its applications across various domains.
